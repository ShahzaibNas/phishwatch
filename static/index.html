FAQ

Questions, answered

How does PhishWatch find lookalike domains?

We generate realistic variations of your domain — typos, character swaps, and homograph (non-Latin lookalike) permutations — then check which ones actually resolve in DNS and serve a live website. Standing, live lookalikes are the ones worth worrying about.

What are Certificate Transparency logs?

They're public, append-only logs that record every SSL/TLS certificate issued on the web. Because nearly every phishing site gets a certificate, these logs act as a near-real-time feed of new domains going live — which is exactly what we monitor.

What does the risk score mean?

Each lookalike is scored out of 7 based on the signals we detect: whether it's a known typo/homograph pattern, whether it resolves in DNS, and whether it serves a live web server. Higher scores mean a more credible, active threat.

Is the scan really free?

Yes. You can scan your domain right now with no account and no payment. It's designed to give you an immediate, honest picture of who may be impersonating your brand.
